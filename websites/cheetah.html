Cheetah Facts

Species and Subspecies

Species

  • Acinonyx jubatus

Sub-Species

  • Southeast African Cheetah
  • Asiatic Cheetah
  • Northeast African Cheetah
  • Northwest African Cheetah

Habitats

  • Open grasslands
  • Savannas
  • Shrublands

Diet

  • Cheetahs are Carnivores
  • Cheetahs will eat small antelope, steenbok, springbok, duikers, impala, and gazelles
  • Cheetahs also like to hunt birds and rabbits

Early Life

  • A litter of Cheetah cubs usually includes three to five cubs
  • At birth, cubs weigh 150 to 400 g (0.33-0.88 lbs.)
  • Cheetahs usually stay with their mother for about 18 months
  • Cheetah cubs usually open their eyes after four to eleven days
  • Cheetah cubs usually start eating meat when they are three weeks old
  • Cheetah cubs love to play fight with their siblings

Personality

  • Cheetahs rely on tall grasses for camouflage when hunting
  • Cheetahs usually hunt during the day
  • Male Cheetahs are typically solitary animals usually only living with Cheetahs from their own litter whereas female Cheetahs typically live alone or with their cubs
  • A group of male Cheetahs living together is known as a coalitions
  • Cheetahs stalk their prey, approaching to within about 50 feet before dashing out from cover and sprinting at the targeted animals
  • In the 16th century, emperors and other royalty hunted gazelles with trained cheetahs.

Features

  • Cheetahs are usually 3.5 to 4.5 feet long not including their tails
  • A Cheetahs tail is usually around 30 inches long
  • An adult Cheetah usually weighs 110 to 143 lbs (50-65kg)
  • A sprinting Cheetah can reach up to 45 miles per hour within 2.5 seconds.
  • Cheetahs have a top speed of up to 70 miles per hour
  • Cheetahs cannot roar, but they can purr
  • A Cheetah will also chirp
  • Cheetahs are the fastest mammals on Earth
  • They have a keen sense of hearing, sight and smell
  • There were a few Cheetahs found who exhibit distinctive blotches and stripes in their coat thanks to a rare genetic mutation. These Cheetahs are known as king Cheetahs
  • Cheetahs spots are all unique and are often used to identify one another

Population

  • There are only about 7,100 cheetahs left in the wild
  • The species is mainly threatened by the illegal wildlife trade, loss of prey due to overhunting, and poaching
  • Cheetahs usually live for 10 to 12 years in the wild, and can live up to 17 years in captivity
  • The cheetah, Acinonyx jubatus, is the sole member of its genus - all other species have become extinct
  • for some unknown reason, at least 99 percent of the entire world population of cheetahs died in a very short time frame
  • Cheetahs were first discovered in 1610
